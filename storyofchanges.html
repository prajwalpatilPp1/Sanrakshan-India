Doulabi

From a shy, withdrawn girl facing trauma and family hardship, Doulabi transformed into a confident, high-achieving student at Sanrakshan Rainbow Home. With counselling, activities, and consistent support, she excelled academically (93% marks), built strong bonds with peers and staff, and became a role model for younger girls, including her sister Yasmin. Her journey shows the power of love, care, and a safe environment in unlocking a child’s true potential.

Anjum

Anjum arrived at the Sanrakshan Rainbow Home at age 7, frightened and unable to trust others after being found begging on the streets despite having parents unable to care for her. Initially sensitive and insecure, she struggled to adjust to the new environment. Through steady counselling, emotional support, and a structured routine, Anjum gradually felt safe, began attending school regularly, and developed confidence. She now participates in activities, studies with enthusiasm, and has become a cheerful, connected, and affectionate child. Her progress reflects the transformative power of consistent care and support.

Lakshmi R

Lakshmi R, a 21-year-old, grew up in a large, financially struggling family after losing her father early in life. She joined the home five years ago while studying in 8th standard, seeking stability and support. With consistent care, guidance, and encouragement, Lakshmi rebuilt her confidence and stayed focused on her education. She completed her SSLC in 2023 and later finished her PUC under the Future Program, reflecting steady progress and determination. Grateful for the emotional and educational support she received, Lakshmi now stands as an inspiring example of perseverance. She aims to build a stable career and hopes to motivate other children facing similar challenges.

Mamatha

Mamatha, who came from a financially challenged family with an ailing father, joined Sanrakshan Rainbow Home in 8th grade, struggling with academics and emotional stress. Through consistent counselling, activity-based engagement in dance and cultural programs, and supportive staff check-ins, she gradually gained confidence, discovered her strengths, and developed leadership skills. Today, she has completed 10th standard with excellent marks, actively participates in school and hostel activities, and serves as a role model for her peers—a true example of how encouragement and structured support can transform resilience into success.
