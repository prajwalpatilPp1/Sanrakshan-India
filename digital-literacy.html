Digital Literacy

Bridging the digital divide for children in government schools and underserved communities.

Digital Classroom Program

Objectives of the Program

  • Empower Through Education: To create a knowledgeable and empowered India by providing access to quality education for all.
  • Leverage Volunteer Passion: To connect motivated and skilled volunteers with students, ensuring quality educational support, especially for children in rural areas.
  • Bridge the Educational Divide: To reduce disparities in education by offering digital learning opportunities to underserved and rural communities.
  • Enhance School Infrastructure: To support government schools in improving infrastructure and teaching resources, thereby raising the overall standard of education.

Why Need This Program

Many government schools in Karnataka face challenges such as poor infrastructure, lack of basic facilities, and limited access to digital learning tools. Children from marginalized and rural communities are most affected, often missing out on quality education. Poor school conditions and outdated teaching methods can demotivate students, leading to low engagement and higher dropout rates. To address this, Sanrakshan has partnered with eVidyaloka to bring digital classrooms to government schools. Through this initiative, students gain access to interactive lessons, trained volunteers, and modern learning resources. Combined with efforts to improve school infrastructure, the program aims to create a supportive and motivating environment, helping children continue their education and reach their full potential.

Intervention Strategies

1

Identification of Schools with Inadequate Infrastructure

Government schools in rural and marginalized regions are assessed to identify those lacking essential facilities, digital learning tools, and supportive learning environments. Priority is given to schools where poor infrastructure is directly affecting student engagement and academic performance.

2

Establishment of Digital Classrooms

Digital classrooms are set up in selected remote government schools, equipped with the necessary hardware and connectivity to enable virtual learning.

3

Deployment of Skilled Volunteer

Teachers Trained volunteers from across the country and abroad conduct interactive lessons in the regional language for two hours per week, ensuring strong academic support in core subjects and emerging fields.

4

Strengthening Formal Education Delivery

Partnerships are developed with government schools to enhance learning outcomes through structured and monitored academic support aligned with school curricula.

5

Integration of Digital Literacy and STEM Learning

Students are introduced to computer literacy, coding skills, and foundational concepts of Artificial Intelligence alongside Science, Mathematics, and English.

6

Provision of Comprehensive Teaching Resources

Volunteer teachers are supported with structured teaching aids, including lesson plans, presentations, and multimedia resources to deliver engaging and effective learning sessions.

7

Holistic Skill Development

Life skills and leadership activities are included to support the all-round growth of students, helping them build confidence, critical thinking, and communication skills.

8

Continuous Monitoring and Improvement

Progress is routinely evaluated to strengthen implementation, improve outcomes, and ensure that students receive sustainable and impactful learning experiences.

Impact of The Program

The program has contributed to a sustained reduction in school dropout rates by creating a more engaging and supportive learning environment. Students have gained improved digital literacy skills through regular exposure to technology-enabled learning. Over time, these advancements have enhanced their academic performance and expanded their future opportunities, contributing to better employability and long-term socio-economic development for the beneficiaries.
