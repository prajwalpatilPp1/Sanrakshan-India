3. What We Do

1️⃣    Childcare & Development
We provide safe, nurturing environments where children can grow with dignity and access the support they need for holistic development. Through Sanrakshan Rainbow Home for Girls and Sankalpa Makkala Dhama for Boys, we rescue children from vulnerable circumstances and offer them comprehensive care, education, and emotional support to help them build brighter futures.

•	Sanrakshan Rainbow Home: 
•	A residential home for girls offering care, education, counselling, and life skills.
•	Operating for the past 4 years at the Govt Primary & High School, Jooganahalli, Rajajinagar
•	Provides shelter, education, medical & counselling support to 60 girls (ages 7–18) from vulnerable backgrounds.
•	Sankalpa Makkala Dhama:
•	A residential home for boys offering care, education, counselling, and life skills.
•	Operating since November 2024 at the Government Lower Primary School, Chikkangamangala.
•	Provides shelter, education, medical & counselling support to 40 boys (ages 7–18) from vulnerable backgrounds.
Key Interventions & Activities of Sanrakshan Rainbow Home & Sankalpa Makkala Dhama:
•	Nutritious meals and remedial education support in evenings
•	Children’s Committees for education, food, health, discipline, cleaning, sports, welcome, and purchasing – fostering responsibility & participation.
•	Celebrations of birthdays and special days with sweets, gifts, and joy.
•	Daily yoga & meditation, monthly health check-ups, and health & safety education.
•	Educational outings (Planetarium, museum, aquarium, etc.) for exposure and learning.
•	Child Protection Committee to prevent/address abuse.
•	Individual Care Plans and case management for each child.
•	Quarterly Parents’ Day to reassure families about children’s progress.
•	Weekly counselling sessions (individual & group) by a professional counsellor.
•	Monthly CWC presentation for monitoring of children in care.
•	Weekend online Conversational English classes for skill building.
•	Annual summer camp with external support.

•	Community-Based Care (CBC): 
•	Support for vulnerable children and families below the poverty line and in marginalised communities.
•	Assistance includes Education, counselling, motivation, and personality development.
•	Children nurtured within families will be placed with parents or extended family whenever possible.
•	Kinship Care placement with extended family/close family friends to reduce disruption in education and social life.
•	Parents/caregivers receive support for children’s educational, health, and emotional needs until age 18.
•	Regular monitoring and engagement with caregivers to ensure the child’s well-being and minimise trauma.
•	Caregivers are supported for access to quality education, holistic development, and emotional stability.
•	Adoption cases tracked with comprehensive records and timely follow-ups.
•	Rainbow Community Care Learning Centre (RCCLC): 
•	Providing bridge courses and remedial classes for children from the Kole Basava community who dropped out of school.
•	Helping children reintegrate into nearby government schools.
•	Offering evening remedial classes for sustained academic support.
•	Conducting life skills sessions for holistic development.
•	Forming Parents Collectives to guide families on entitlements and schemes.
•	Assisting families in securing essential documents like Aadhar, bank accounts, voter IDs, pensions, and ration cards.
2️⃣   Child Protection
We work in close coordination with the child welfare system and law enforcement to rescue, protect, and support children in hazardous or abusive situations.
•	Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU) Program
•	Sanrakshan collaborates with 31 police stations in Bengaluru, along with the Directorate of Child Protection and the Department of Police.
•	Rescue operations cover children from construction sites, hotels, domestic conflicts, abandonment, homelessness, street situations, begging, abuse, trafficking, and kidnapping.
•	Support to SJPU includes counselling children in conflict with the law and those in need of care and protection.
•	Assistance in legal procedures, case studies, and coordination with SJPU and the Child Welfare Committee.
•	The project targets at least 150 children to receive counselling and legal aid across Central and West Divisions.
•	Community awareness programs on child care and protection, boosting children’s confidence, and supporting them in legal cases.
3️⃣    Skill Development
Empowering adolescents with the tools they need to transition into independent adulthood through career readiness and life-skills training.
•	Vocational Training & Job Placement: Connecting young adults with skill-building opportunities and employment support.
•	Future Program for Young Adults (FPYA): 
•	Children leaving Child Care Institutions at 18 often lack social, emotional, and financial support to live independently.
•	The Futures Program prepares them for a smooth transition into stable, responsible adult life.
•	It empowers youth through skill development and sustained aftercare support for personal and professional growth.
•	The program addresses challenges of unemployment, lack of skills, and weak support systems.
•	Support includes career counselling, resume-building workshops, and partnerships with businesses for job placements.
•	Collaboration with educational institutions provides continuing education opportunities and scholarships for higher studies or advanced training.
•	Mentorship connects youth with professionals in their fields and ensures ongoing guidance for career growth.
4️⃣    Digital Literacy
Bridging the digital divide for children in government schools and underserved communities.
•	Digital Classrooms Initiative:
With the support of eVidyaloka and in our commitment to ensuring equitable access to technology, digital education, and improved infrastructure, we have launched the Digital Classroom Program in selected government schools. Through this initiative, we are setting up digital infrastructure and providing students with access to digital learning tools and resources. The program empowers children to build essential digital literacy, gain confidence, and actively participate in the technology-driven future.
Currently, three Government Schools are part of the program:
•	Government High School, Ravothanahalli
•	Government Higher Primary School, Avalagurki
•	Government Higher Primary School, Srirampura
