Child Protection

We work in close coordination with the child welfare system and law enforcement to rescue, protect, and support children in hazardous or abusive situations. Through our Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU) Program, we provide comprehensive support to children in conflict with the law and those in need of care and protection.

Special Juvenile Police Unit Program (SJPU)

  • Sanrakshan collaborates with 31 police stations in Bengaluru, along with the Directorate of Child Protection and the Department of Police.
  • Rescue operations cover children from construction sites, hotels, domestic conflicts, abandonment, homelessness, street situations, begging, abuse, trafficking, and kidnapping.
  • Support to SJPU includes counselling children in conflict with the law and those in need of care and protection.
  • Assistance in legal procedures, case studies, and coordination with SJPU and the Child Welfare Committee.
  • The project targets at least 150 children to receive counselling and legal aid across Central and West Divisions.
  • Community awareness programs on child care and protection, boosting children's confidence, and supporting them in legal cases.

Objectives of the Program

Rescue and Protect Vulnerable Children ▼
To identify, rescue, and safeguard children who are in vulnerable situations including those in conflict with the law, victims of abuse, trafficking, and exploitation, and to provide essential care, protection, and comprehensive support.
Legal Support & Child Welfare Coordination ▼
To provide legal assistance, case management, and coordination with SJPU, Child Welfare Committee, and law enforcement agencies to ensure justice and protection of child rights.
Counselling & Psychosocial Support ▼
To offer professional counselling services to children in conflict with the law and those in need of care and protection, addressing trauma, behavioral issues, and emotional wellbeing.
Community Awareness & Prevention ▼
To conduct community awareness programs on child protection, child rights, and prevention of child abuse, trafficking, and exploitation, fostering a protective environment for all children.

Why Need This Program

In Bengaluru, thousands of children face vulnerability due to poverty, family breakdown, migration, and exploitation. Many children are found in street situations, engaged in child labor, begging, or at risk of trafficking and abuse. Children in conflict with the law often lack proper legal representation and psychosocial support. The existing child protection system requires strengthened coordination between police, child welfare committees, and NGOs. The SJPU program addresses this critical gap by providing immediate rescue, legal aid, counselling, and rehabilitation services. Through collaboration with 31 police stations and government departments, we ensure that vulnerable children receive timely intervention, protection, and support to rebuild their lives with dignity and hope.

Key Interventions

1. Rescue Operations

  • Coordinate with police stations to identify and rescue children from vulnerable situations.
  • Immediate intervention for children in construction sites, hotels, streets, and hazardous environments.
  • Emergency response for cases of abuse, trafficking, and exploitation.

2. Legal Support & Case Management

  • Provide legal assistance and representation for children in conflict with the law.
  • Coordinate with Child Welfare Committee for proper case documentation and follow-up.
  • Ensure children's rights are protected throughout legal proceedings.

3. Counselling Services

  • Individual and group counselling for children in need of care and protection.
  • Trauma-informed care for victims of abuse and exploitation.
  • Behavioral therapy and emotional support for children in conflict with the law.

4. Community Awareness

  • Conduct awareness programs on child protection and child rights.
  • Sensitization sessions for parents, community members, and stakeholders.
  • Prevention campaigns against child labor, trafficking, and abuse.

5. Coordination & Networking

  • Collaborate with 31 police stations, Directorate of Child Protection, and Department of Police.
  • Regular coordination meetings with SJPU and Child Welfare Committee.
  • Network with government agencies, NGOs, and community organizations for comprehensive child protection.
