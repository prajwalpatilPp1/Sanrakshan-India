Future Program for Young Adults (FPYA)

Objectives of the Program

  • Support Transition to Independent Adulthood: Facilitate a smooth and supported transition for care leavers as they move toward independence.
  • Secure and Safe Living Arrangements: Ensure access to stable, safe, and appropriate housing during and after the transition from care.
  • Capacity Building and Career Guidance: Provide sustained support in developing life skills, career readiness, and employability.
  • Education and Mentoring Access: Promote informed decision-making and provide guidance to help participants continue and succeed in education.
  • Assistance with Identity and Entitlement Documents: Support care leavers in obtaining essential identity, legal, and entitlement documents.
  • Health and Nutrition Support: Ensure continued access to healthcare services, mental health support, and nutritional guidance.
  • Emotional Security and Well-being: Provide consistent emotional support to foster resilience, well-being, and self-confidence before, during, and after the transition to adulthood.

Why Need This Program

Transitioning from care to independent adulthood is a critical stage for young adults leaving CCI. At eighteen, they are legally on their own, but their sense of identity and stability is still fragile. Without ongoing support, setbacks can erase years of progress. The Futures Program bridges this gap, providing mentoring, education, emotional support, and guidance from ages sixteen to twenty-three, preparing them for a stable, independent, and responsible adult life.



Key Interventions

Life Exposure

  • Provide opportunities for children to interact with people from diverse backgrounds.
  • Bimonthly sessions with ordinary individuals who exemplify values and effective communication.
  • Interview-style sessions to draw practical life lessons.

Life Plan Process

  • Help young adults visualize aspirations and create realistic life plans.
  • Workshops, mind-mapping, and guidance from peers, mentors, and alumni.
  • Life plans reviewed periodically to track progress and update goals.

Life Education

  • Prepare young adults for independent living, work, and societal roles.
  • Literacy, numeracy, English, and life skills training.
  • Workshops on financial, digital, legal literacy, identity, and professional management.
  • Volunteering, internships, and driving lessons for practical experience.

Continued Education

  • Support completion of secondary education and entry into higher studies.
  • Map educational opportunities, ensure admissions, and monitor academic progress.

Part-time Jobs

  • Encourage financial independence through safe, flexible work.
  • Build partnerships for suitable placements and alternative arrangements when needed.

Social Security & Entitlements

  • Ensure possession of identity, legal, and welfare documents.
  • Support physical and mental health, including medical coverage up to age 21.

Living Arrangements

  • Assess family suitability and reintegrate when safe.
  • Provide managed youth homes or alternative independent living options.
  • Provide transition stipends and guidance for independent living.

Mentor, Peer & Alumni Platforms

  • Provide regular mentoring and role models for guidance.
  • Organize peer meetings and alumni networks for support and advice.

Special Needs

  • Identify young adults with disabilities or crises.
  • Provide long-term or short-term tailored support and monitor progress.

Intervention Strategies

Impact of The Program

The program fosters increased confidence, resilience, and emotional well-being among participants, equipping them to navigate the challenges of independent adulthood. Through targeted skill-building and career guidance, it enhances life skills, employability, and financial independence, while supporting higher rates of continued education and successful career placements. Participants benefit from safe, stable living arrangements and ensured access to social security and essential entitlements. The program strengthens support networks by connecting young adults with mentors, peers, and alumni, providing ongoing guidance and encouragement for personal and professional growth.

How Your Support Can Make a Difference

The Future Program for Young Adults (FPYA) provides holistic support to youth aged 15 to 23 as they prepare for independent living, covering essential needs such as education, life-skills training, health care, reskilling, personal expenses, and hostel fees for 18+ young adults who require safe and supervised living arrangements. With an annual budget of Rs. 4,76,100, the program ensures that every young adult receives the guidance, stability, and opportunities needed to progress confidently toward a secure and self-reliant future.
