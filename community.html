Community-Based Care Program

The Community-Based Care (CBC) Program, initiated in 2021 as an alternative care approach, has shown remarkable results in reaching the most vulnerable children while optimizing resource use.

The CBC program is implemented through the following two interventions

Family-Based Care

Rainbow Community Care Learning Centre (RCCLC)



Family-Based Care

Objectives of the Program

  • Provision of Comprehensive Care for Children – To ensure children in the community have access to essential services, including food security, education, life skills, health care, and emotional support through learning centres, while shelter is provided by their parents.
  • Capacity Building of Parents and Community – To strengthen the skills and knowledge of parents and community members so that children have access to safety mechanisms, protection, and supportive environments at home and in the community.
  • Family Strengthening and Awareness Programs – To empower families through awareness initiatives on social issues, children’s rights, entitlements, and joint child care plans, fostering responsible parenting and sustainable child care practices.
  • Building Strong and Resilient Communities – To create stronger communities capable of safeguarding children, supporting families, and ensuring the holistic development and well-being of children in the community.

Why We Need This Program

Safe Reintegration

Children reintegrated from Sanrakshan Rainbow Home need support to transition safely and successfully back to family life.

Financial Support

Parents may need additional financial and other support to ensure proper education, nutrition, and overall care for their children.

Emotional Challenges

Children experience emotional, social, and developmental challenges after leaving institutional care requiring specialized guidance.

Family Guidance

Families require guidance and structured support to meet children's needs effectively and prevent neglect or abuse.

Prevention & Monitoring

Structured monitoring and preparation help prevent family disruption and ensure children's safety and well-being.

Our Solution

By strengthening family bonds and ensuring access to education, health, and psychosocial services, the Family-Based Care program promotes long-term well-being and stability of children.

Key Interventions

Comprehensive Care

  • Covers multiple dimensions of child well-being: food security, education, life skills, health, social security groups, and community child protection mechanisms.
  • Integrates these components to ensure holistic development and protection of the child within their family and community.
  • Weekly visits during initial reintegration and fortnightly thereafter using a checklist to monitor health, hygiene, nutrition, schooling, and overall wellbeing.
  • Regular phone contact is maintained with the child and family for continuous updates.

Family Strengthening

  • Focuses on empowering the entire family, not just the child.
  • Engages parents and caregivers through awareness on social issues, child safety, access to entitlements, and joint childcare planning.
  • Promotes stronger communities that collectively support children and improve overall family well-being.
  • Aligns with RHP’s long-term goal of family strengthening.

Shelter and Safety

  • Parents provide shelter, while the program builds their capacity to ensure children’s safety.
  • Helps families and communities develop mechanisms for a secure environment for growth and protection.

Individual Childcare Plans

  • Each child receives a personalized childcare plan covering health, education, nutrition, protection, and overall development.
  • Ensures a tailored approach to every child’s well-being.

Food and Nutrition

  • Supports children with balanced and nutritious meals while living with parents.
  • Provides a monthly nutrition kit co-designed by city networks, implementing partners, and City Desk.

Hygiene and Health Care

  • Quarterly health camps for children, families, and the community.
  • Children have access to healthcare support whenever needed.
  • Regular awareness sessions on personal hygiene and grooming.
  • Monthly hygiene kits are provided.
  • Community health programs address communicable and non-communicable diseases.

Education and Life Skills

  • Enrols children in nearby government schools.
  • Provides after-school tuition, academic support, and remedial learning.
  • CBC coordinators ensure focused support for each child.
  • Conducts life skills sessions to enhance confidence and decision-making.
  • Identifies and nurtures individual talents, linking children to expert organizations.
  • Includes learning loss assessments and mental health monitoring.

Intervention Strategies – Reintegration Timeline

Step 1 — Assessment of Reintegrated Children

Every child returning from residential care is assessed based on structured criteria ensuring safety, wellbeing and education.

Step 2 — Eligibility Criteria

All the following conditions must be met:

  • Both parents are alive and family situation improved
  • Parents are willing and capable with FBC support
  • Child is willing to return home and study
  • Safe housing with toilet, water, electricity
  • Parents encourage education; no child labour or early marriage
  • No risk of abuse or exploitation
  • Stable livelihood available
Step 3 — Verification & Recommendation

A committee of 3–4 members conducts a home visit and assesses conditions using the Social Investigation Report. They provide reintegration recommendation.

Step 4 — Parental Commitment

Parents sign a formal undertaking agreeing to all non-negotiable aspects of child safety, development and support.

Impact of The Program

The Family-Based Care program has transformed the lives of 20 reintegrated children by replacing institutional care with safe, nurturing family environments. Children now experience stability, emotional security, and continuous developmental support. Families and communities have become stronger protectors of child well-being, creating a lasting support system that ensures every child can grow, thrive, and look toward a brighter future.
