Why Need This Program

Essential support for successful family reintegration

The Family-Based Care program is essential to ensure that children reintegrated from the Sanrakshan Rainbow Home into their families can transition safely and successfully to family life. While parents often have the ability to provide shelter and security, they may need additional financial and other support to ensure their children receive proper education, nutrition, and overall care. Many children experience emotional, social, and developmental challenges after leaving institutional care, and families may require guidance to meet these needs effectively. This program provides structured support, monitoring, and preparation for both children and families, helping to prevent neglect, abuse, or family disruption. By strengthening family bonds and ensuring access to education, health, and psychosocial services, the Family-Based Care program promotes the long-term well-being and stability of children, making it a vital initiative for successful reintegration.

Reintegration Journey

Four-step process ensuring safe and successful family reunification

Step 1 — Assessment of Reintegrated Children

Every child returning from residential care is assessed based on structured criteria ensuring safety, wellbeing and education. Comprehensive evaluation includes family readiness, child's emotional state, and environmental factors.

1

Step 2 — Eligibility Criteria

All the following conditions must be met:

  • Both parents are alive and family situation improved
  • Parents are willing and capable with FBC support
  • Child is willing to return home and study
  • Safe housing with toilet, water, electricity
  • Parents encourage education; no child labour or early marriage
  • No risk of abuse or exploitation
  • Stable livelihood available
2

Step 3 — Verification & Recommendation

A committee of 3–4 members conducts a home visit and assesses conditions using the Social Investigation Report. They provide reintegration recommendation based on comprehensive evaluation of family environment and support systems.

3

Step 4 — Parental Commitment

Parents sign a formal undertaking agreeing to all non-negotiable aspects of child safety, development and support. This commitment ensures accountability and establishes clear expectations for the child's care and wellbeing.

4

Rainbow Community Care Learning Centre (RCCLC)

About the Program

The Rainbow Community Care Learning Centre (RCCLC) is a community-based after-school program designed to support vulnerable children living with their families. Operating in the evenings, RCCLC provides a safe, nurturing space where children receive educational support, nutritious meals, life skills training, and holistic development opportunities. The program bridges the gap between school and home, ensuring children from economically disadvantaged backgrounds have access to quality education, health services, and protective care while remaining within their family and community environment.

Objectives of the Program

Educational Support & Academic Excellence ▼
To provide after-school remedial education, homework assistance, and bridge learning programs that help children excel academically and stay enrolled in formal schooling.
Nutrition & Health Support ▼
To ensure children receive nutritious evening meals, regular health check-ups, and hygiene education, addressing malnutrition and health challenges faced by vulnerable children.
Life Skills & Holistic Development ▼
To build confidence, critical thinking, communication skills, and emotional intelligence through structured life skills sessions, creative activities, and talent development programs.
Child Protection & Safe Environment ▼
To create a safe, protective space where children are monitored, supported, and protected from abuse, exploitation, and neglect while fostering community awareness on child rights and protection.

Why Need This Program

Many children from economically disadvantaged families face significant barriers to education and development. Parents working long hours in informal sectors cannot provide adequate supervision, educational support, or nutritious meals during critical after-school hours. Children are often left unsupervised, vulnerable to exploitation, or engaged in child labor. Poor learning outcomes, malnutrition, and lack of safe spaces further compound their challenges. RCCLC addresses these gaps by providing a structured, supportive environment during evening hours, ensuring children receive education, nutrition, protection, and holistic development opportunities. By keeping children engaged in positive activities and providing family support, RCCLC prevents school dropout, child labor, and exploitation while promoting academic success and overall wellbeing.

Key Interventions

1. After-School Education

  • Remedial classes and homework assistance for all subjects.
  • Bridge learning programs for children with learning gaps.
  • Individual attention and personalized learning plans.
  • Library access and reading programs to enhance literacy.

2. Nutrition & Health

  • Nutritious evening meals for all children attending RCCLC.
  • Regular health check-ups and medical support.
  • Hygiene education and personal grooming sessions.
  • Health camps and vaccination drives for children and families.

3. Life Skills & Development

  • Life skills training on communication, decision-making, and problem-solving.
  • Creative activities including art, music, dance, and sports.
  • Talent identification and development programs.
  • Confidence building and leadership development.

4. Child Protection

  • Safe, supervised environment during after-school hours.
  • Child protection awareness and safety education.
  • Monitoring and reporting of child abuse or exploitation.
  • Counselling support for children facing challenges.

5. Family & Community Engagement

  • Regular parent meetings and progress updates.
  • Family counselling and support services.
  • Community awareness programs on child rights and education.
  • Linkages to government schemes and entitlements.
